Faculty

Here you find presentation of the faculty at the Department of Media Technology and Graphic Arts at CSC.

MARIANN BERGGREN, ekonomiadministratör: Leverantörsfakturor, utlägg, kundfakturor, budget och rapporter, EU-projekt, reseräkningar, kontorsmaterial, arkivering, sjukanmälan, arvoden, stipendier, utlämning av TRITA-nummer till forskningsrapporter m.m.

LEIF DAHLBERG received his PhD in Comparative Literature from Stockholms universitet. Before teaching Communication and Media studies at KTH he taught as senoir lecturer of Comparative Literature at Linköpings universitet and Stockholms universitet. He has been visiting lecturer/professor at Beijing Daxue (Peking University) and at Södertörns högskolat. He teaches media and theoretical texts that range from the eighteenth to the twenty-first century. He has written on German Romanticism, European Modernism, Narratology, Media history, Law and Literature, Advertising, and New media. Web site: http://www.csc.kth.se/~dahlberg

HANNES EBNER is currently performing his PhD studies at the department of Media Technology and Graphic Arts. He holds a Diploma Engineer degree in Telecommunication from the University of Applied Sciences St. Pölten in Austria and an MSc degree in Information Systems from KTH. His research focus lies on loose and non-invasive collaboration, metadata interoperability and Semantic Web technologies, in particular applications of Linked Open (Meta)Data in the context of Technology Enhanced Learning. He is also one of the leading architects and developers behind numerous Open Source projects, such as the collaboration server Collaborilla, the concept browser Conzilla, the SCAM metadata and resource management framework, and the web-based electronic portfolio application Confolio.

KATARINA ELEVANT is a Ph.D. student with a background in Meteorology and Physics from Stockholm University and with experience from working as meteorologist at the Swedish Institute of Meteorology and Hydrology (SMHI). She is responsible for starting up and managing the first private weather service in Sweden, a subsidiary of Foreca Ltd. Elevant is a market expert for weather services and, beside management and environmental consultancy, has previously worked on the development of weather services for transport, energy and media. The latter has recently become a special area of interest in Elevant’s research, in particular how social media will affect weather services of the future, not the least considering the social impacts of extreme weather. The research is focused on customization and personalization of weather information, with applications such as early-warning services and weather services in daily life, tested through the ”share weather” system (www.shareweather.com) developed within the project. Elevant’s project is funded by Vinnova. The project is also supported by the city of Stockholm, SL and Sveriges Radio (Radio Stockholm P4).

NILS ENLUND is professor emeritus and has a background in Computer Science from the Helsinki University of Technology in Finland but has since 1970 devoted his work and research to applications of information technology in media production and consumption. He has earlier served, among other positions, as researcher and assistant professor at Helsinki University of Technology, development manager at Typlan Oy, Finland, research director of IFRA, Germany, and professor of Text and Image Processing at KTH. He has held his current position since 1991. His current research interests include mediated presence, multichannel media production, media development, and media in art and performance. He is chairman of the scientific committee of IARIGAI, vice president of the Swedish Academy of Verbovisual Information, and member of the board of the KTH Center for Opera and Technology.

LEIF HANDBERG is senior lecturer in Media Technology. He is teaching the introductory course at the M Sc program in Media Technology, the specialisation course in Telepresence Production and is also responsible for the individual course in Media Technology. His research interest is in presence production and has worked in several such projects, at present together with museums concerning mediation of cultural heritage. He is also responsible for the use of the reactor hall as an experimental performance space and to build a platform for collaboration with artistic schools in the Stockholm area. Leif Handbergs motto is: "bridge builder, problem solver, facilitator, fun maker"

BJÖRN HEDIN has a background in computer science, and is now teaching interactive media and web technologies at KTH. Currently he is mainly responsible for the courses DM2517 XML for publishing and DM1578 Program integrating course. His research interests focus on technology enhanced learning (TEL) with a special emphasis on mobile learning (mLearning). He is also interested in mobility in general, location based services, social media, quality assessment in higher education, and improvement of the media technology programme. Part of his funding is from "KTH Virtual Campus". Björn will be part-time on parental leave during the spring 2010 and full-time during the autumn.

STEFAN HRASTINSKI received his PhD from Lund University, Sweden, in 2007. Currently, he is an Assistant Professor in Media Technology at KTH Royal Institute of Technology and Research Fellow at Uppsala University. Stefan has been a visiting scholar at Universidad Tecnólogica Nacional, Argentina, and Queensland University of Technology. His research focuses on learning and collaboration media in educational and organizational settings. Stefan has published many journal articles, books, book chapters and conference papers. His work has appeared in journals such as Computers & Education, Information & Management and Interactive Learning Environments. Recently, Stefan's book "Online education: an introduction" (in Swedish) was published. More information is available on www.anst.uu.se/stehr914.

EVA-MARIA JACOBSSON is a PhD student working on a thesis with the title “Gender Issues in New Communication Media” to be completed in 2010. She did her masters degree in Media and Communication at Goldsmiths College, University of London. Her research interests include new media and gender issues in new media communication; media economics - issues of news media independence, quality and competition; efficient environmental inspection and enforcement (EEIE). Jacobsson is communication strategist for the project EEIE which is funded by the Swedish Environmental Protection Agency (www.effektivmiljotillsyn.se). Recent publications: “The impact of market competition on journalistic performance” (with A. Jacobsson, L. B. Becker, T. Vlad, C.A. Hollifield, C.), paper presented at the conference of the International Association for Media and Communication Research, Stockholm, July 2008; “Is more always better? Examining the adverse effects of competition on media performance” (with L. B. Becker, C. A. Hollifield, Adam Jacobsson and Tudor Vlad), Journalism Studies, Vol. 10 No. 3 (2009), pp 368-385; ”Quality of news” (with Adam Jacobsson) in W. Donsbach (ed), The Blackwell International Encyclopedia of Communication (2008).

DANIEL JOHANSSON, PhD Student in Computer Science (www.danieljohansson.se). Daniel has a background from the music industry, during eight years he was a part of the largest music festival in Sweden, the Hultsfred festival, and worked as a teacher at the educations in Hultsfred. Since 2005 he have been studying how the music industry is changing as a result of the shift in distribution technologies. His research has been focusing on systems for collecting and dividing revenues from online music services and he has been following the changes in consumer behaviour related to digital music usage. He is also doing research as a part of the large European project P2P-Next (www.p2p-next.org) that are developing a new open source platform for P2P video distribution over the net. Partners in the consortium are, among others, BBC, Technische Universiteit Delft, Markenfilm, STMicroelectronics, University of Klagenfurt, European Broadcasting Union, VTT - Technical Research Centre of Finland and Pioneer. One of his current research projects have resulted in a new start-up company, TrendMaze (www.trendmaze.com), that are using the Music Intelligence Mashup System, one of the models that Daniel have developed for his PhD thesis.

TOMMO RETI, M.Sc. (Tech.), is an experienced top executive and project leader. He founded and sold two software companies in 1996-2001, responsible for strategy, R&D, VC funding, and executive stafﬁng. Before exiting through MBOs, he directed over 60 employees on two continents. He invented the Digital Content Distribution Management System DiMaS, which won the title ”The Best e-Business Application in Finland” in 2004. He is a doctoral candidate at GEBSI in Finland and at KTH, and recently he spent two years at the UC Berkeley, USA. As a project leader at KTH, he coordinates the joint research project Next Generation Media with TeliaSonera.

JOHAN STENBERG is working as a part time visiting professor during 2009/2010. He received his PhD in Media Technology at KTH in 1997 and continued with a post doc at KTH. During 1999-2007 he founded and worked as an executive in the spinoff company MWM Media Workflow Management AB. In parallel to the work in the media industry, Johan Stenberg acts as supervisor, examinor, and project manager in reasearch projects at KTH. Since 2007 he works as technical director in the Bonnier-company Bold Printing Group AB.

CECILIA TELJAS is a Ph.D. student in Media Technology at the Royal Institute of Technology in Stockholm, Sweden. Her area of research is Social Media and the uses of Social Media in companies and organizations. Teljas has a MSc in Mechanical Engineering with specialization in Publishing Technology, and prior to her doctoral studies she had worked as research engineer in a project for IFRA (a research and service organisation for the news publishing industry), as production manager for the newspapers Aftonbladet and PunktSE and as a Software Engineer at Ericsson.

MARKO TURPEINEN is professor. He has a Ph.D. Degree in Computer Science from Helsinki University of Technology (TKK) and a M.Sc. Degree in Media Arts and Sciences from Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). His academic research addresses issues in customized media, computer-mediated communities, future of gaming, ageing society, and the role of media technology in promoting sustainable development. He has also extensive industrial experience from the media industry, as between 1996 and 2005 he worked in various executive positions at Alma Media Corporation, a Finnish media company.

JORGE LUIS ZAPICO is a PhD candidate sitting at the Centre for Sustainable Communications. He has a multidisciplinary background with a bachelor in Computer Science from Salamanca, Spain and a Master in Sustainable Technology from KTH. His research interests are in the intersection of media technology and sustainability, both studying the negative impact of technologies and their positive effects. His research have a design approach where technological interventions are used proactively for achieving a sustainable society. He is also a web designer, working as freelance, with experience building data driven web applications. More info at: http://www.jorgezapico.com & http://www.sustainablecommunications.org/team/zapico/

RASMUS ÖLME is a PhD student in Media Technology at KTH and in Choreography at the University College of Dance (Stockholm). Originally a dancer, Ölme moved into choreography in 2001. The same year he founded the dance company REFUG in Belgium. In 2007 he moved back to Sweden. Next to the choreographic practice Ölme teaches extensively throughout Europe and his PhD project involves examining the creative potential in combining the artistic and the pedagogical work as well as looking into the relationship between process and product in contemporary dance. The research project is within the field of practical artistic research. Web page: www.refug.eu
