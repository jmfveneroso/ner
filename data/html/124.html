Centre for the Popularization of Mathematics "Signum"

Centre for the Popularization of Mathematics "Signum" is the teaching

unit of the Faculty of Computer Science of Bialystok University of

Technology. It established in May 2008.

The main goals of Signum:

  • to inspire with mathematics, technique and science
  • to show interesting and useful role of mathematics
  • to broaden mathematical knowledge
  • to give equal educational opportunity
  • to make young people interested in mathematical and technological studies

The academic teachers of two Departments: Mathematics and Theoretical

Computer Science are the members of the Centre. They have popularized

mathematics for a few years. Their activity encompasses various

interactive and educational ventures in the city and region, such as

Podlasie Science and Art Festivals or Mathematics Days.

Software Department

Head of the Department

Prof. Leon Bobrowski , DSc, PhD

 The Software Department employs, among others, three professors and eight staff members with PhD degrees. The scientific and research work conducted in the Department is focused on three main areas of interest:

  • data mining and knowledge discovery;
  • parallel and distributed processing;
  • and biomedical computing.

Working within the field of data exploration, the Department research workers develop various techniques based on pseudo-neural networks, evolutionary algorithms, Bayesian networks and fuzzy sets. The second area of interest concerns the use of computational clusters and grid technology especially for data exploration applications. Finally, biomedical computing research concentrates on developing methods of image analysis and modeling as well as medical decision support systems.

The academic teachers of the Department conduct study courses connected with the software creation process with particular attention paid to programming languages and systems design techniques related to databases, applications and also the internet. Moreover, lectures are given on parallel computing, operation systems ,algorithms and statistical models.

Department of Theoretical Computer Science

Head of the Department

Prof. Czesław Bagiński, DSc, PhD

The Department of Theoretical Computer Science employs three professors and six staff members with PhD degrees. The Department staff carries out research work in the field of algebra as well as other mathematical tools used for information processing. The scientific and research work includes:

  • group theory, computer algebra, algebraic aspects of cryptography;
  • algebra – theory of non-commutative rings, Hopf algebras, invariant theory ;
  • modern methods of assessing and managing financial risk;
  • discovery of relationships in multi-relational data;
  • average computation time of probabilistic algorithms;
  • numerical methods;
  • computer aided formalization as well as search and presentation of information in the Mizar system.

Apart from the research work the Department runs, among others, courses on discreet mathematics, algorithms and data structures, mathematical analysis, logic and set theory, econometrics, financial engineering, algebraic aspects of cryptography, algorithms for the analysis of network operational efficiency and reliability, forecasting with elements of technical analysis.

Department of Mathematics

Head of the Department

Prof. Zbigniew Bartosiewicz , DSc, PhD, Eng

 There are 15 faculty members in the Department of Mathematics, 2 with a professor title and 12 with a doctoral degree. The department supports the two courses of studies of the Faculty of Computer Science: computer science and mathematics. The members of the department also teach mathematics to students of most other courses of studies at other faculties. The research is focused on applied mathematics. The following areas are dominating: mathematical control theory (systems on time scales, systems of fractional order, observability, positive systems, systems with delays, differential-algebraic systems), calculus of variations and optimization, differential equations and inclusions, nonsmooth analysis (multifunctions, generalized derivatives), mathematical models in biology and economics.

The Department of Mathematics offers the following courses: mathematical analysis (calculus), differential equations, probability and statistics, abstract and linear algebra, operation research, financial mathematics, mathematical control theory, fuzzy sets, optimization and linear programming.

Many members of the Department of Mathematics are also members of the Center of Popularization of Mathematics "Signum", in the framework of which they give popular lectures on mathematics and take part in science festivals, preparing events related to mathematics.

Department of Information Systems and Computer Networks

Head of the Department

Prof. Jarosław Stepaniuk, DSc, PhD

 The Department of Information Systems and Computer Networks employs, among others, two professors and nine staff members with PhD degrees. They are involved in solving problems connected with artificial intelligence systems as well as current problems related to computer networks. The scientific work includes:

  • artificial intelligence systems;
  • processing methods of uncertain and incomplete information;
  • modelling and simulations;
  • granular computing and rough sets;
  • data mining;
  • intelligent information systems;
  • machine learning;
  • computer networks;
  • emotion detection in music;
  • steganographic and obfuscation techniques,
  • artificial immune systems.

The didactic activities of the Department include such subjects as:

  • artificial intelligence;
  • advanced artificial intelligence systems;
  • internet distributed systems;
  • computer networks ant network programming;
  • compilation techniques;
  • groupware systems;

 

Department of Digital Media and Computer Graphics

Head of the Department

Prof. Zenon A. Sosnowski, DSc, PhD

The Department of was created in the year 2000 but continued the work on digital signal processing started earlier by professor Alexandr Petrovsky and his team. At present the Department employs three professors and 6 staff members with PhD degrees. The Department’s research scope includes such problems as:

  • digital signal processing;
  • speech processing with special emphasis on adaptation and coding of the speech signal;
  • audio processing;
  • signal decomposition techniques, non-uniform transformations in particular;
  • quaternions in digital signal processing;
  • real time systems design in VHDL;
  • processor design;
  • multimedia techniques;
  • multimedia communications technologies;
  • image processing.

The teaching activities conducted by the Department include : computer architecture , real time processes in multimedia systems , digital signal processing, speech processing in multimedia systems, fundamentals of electronic circuits, information processing systems, basics of processor design, 2D and 3D computer graphics.
